# Project Proposal
## Post-Project Sustainability & Utilization Framework for BDSET Centres

**To:** The Managing Director, Bangladesh Hi-Tech Park Authority (BHTPA)  
**From:** ...............................................  
**Date:** ...............................................  
**Subject:** Formal proposal for sustainable operation, utilization, and impact scaling of BDSET centres.

---

## 1. Executive Summary
The `BDSET` programme has established six advanced centres and trained approximately `400+` participants in immersive and emerging technology domains (`AI, VR, DL, ML, XR, AR`). This proposal seeks formal approval to implement a structured post-project sustainability model so that these centres continue as high-impact hubs for training, innovation, employment linkage, and digital public value creation.

The framework is built around four pillars:
1. Operational activation of existing infrastructure and GPU capacity  
2. Institutional rollout of `TCMS` for training governance  
3. Employment and entrepreneurship integration  
4. Long-term sustainability through SOP, monitoring, and OSR

---

## 2. Background and Existing Capacity
### 2.1 Existing Footprint
- `6` BDSET centres are operational.
- Each centre has lab infrastructure including desktop setups.
- The programme has already reached approximately `400+` trainees.

### 2.2 Strategic Assets Available
- Desktop Computer (Type-1 / Type-2)
- Laptop
- DL Mini Super Computer (`NVIDIA RTX A4000`)
- VR Mini Super Computer (`NVIDIA Quadro GV100`)
- VR Headsets
- Interactive Board
- Digital Podium
- Linux Server
- MS Windows Server

---

## 3. Proposal Objectives
1. Ensure uninterrupted post-project operation of all BDSET centres.  
2. Utilize GPU and server resources for training, research, and public digital services support.  
3. Institutionalize `TCMS` as a unified training management and reporting platform.  
4. Link trained beneficiaries with domestic/overseas opportunities and innovation pathways.  
5. Establish a sustainability model through governance and `Own Source Revenue (OSR)`.

---

## 4. Proposed Implementation Model
### 4.1 Infrastructure & GPU Utilization
- Activate all `6 DL` and `6 VR` mini super computers for centre-based practical learning.
- Expand use cases in AI/HPC, government data processing support, and applied research.
- Coordinate with relevant national agencies (including BCC where applicable) for technical collaboration.

### 4.2 TCMS Institutionalization
- Implement `TCMS` as the mandatory system for batch planning, attendance, assessment, certification tracking, and reporting.
- Conduct regular `ToT` to build centre-level operational capability.
- Use TCMS reports for management dashboard and performance review.

### 4.3 Training Continuity and Programme Streams
- Government/non-government official training and ToT.
- Job-oriented digital skills (office productivity, design, web/digital domains).
- Advanced tracks: AI, VR/MR, Immersive Technology, HPC, Professional Freelancing.

### 4.4 Employment & Entrepreneurship Integration
- Domestic placement linkage through employer engagement, job fairs, and sector mapping.
- Overseas pathway support through market alignment, MoU, and competency preparation.
- Startup support in AI/Immersive domains through mentorship and ecosystem connectivity.

### 4.5 International Collaboration
- Implement `International Training Exchange Program` components:
  - Joint training and certification
  - Cross-border research and innovation projects
  - Internship/Fellowship opportunities
  - Global trainer pool development

---

## 5. Governance, Compliance and SOP
### 5.1 Governance Structure
- Lead oversight: `BHTPA`
- Operational coordination: Centre management teams with technical partners
- Inter-agency collaboration: Relevant government and industry bodies

### 5.2 Mandatory Controls
- `24/7` operational readiness for GPU/server infrastructure
- Power continuity (`UPS/IPS`, generator backup)
- Preventive maintenance and security controls
- `SOP` for asset usage, uptime management, incident handling, and quality assurance

---

## 6. Phased Roadmap
### Phase 1: Short-Term (0–6 months)
- Asset handover and role assignment
- GPU lab readiness and uptime setup
- TCMS onboarding and ToT execution

### Phase 2: Mid-Term (6–12 months)
- Full training calendar rollout
- Government/non-government training expansion
- Job fair and placement linkage activation

### Phase 3: Long-Term (1–3 years)
- OSR model stabilization
- Innovation/startup ecosystem expansion
- International exchange and partnership scaling

---

## 7. Risk and Mitigation
| Risk | Mitigation |
|---|---|
| Power instability and downtime | 24/7 power planning, UPS/IPS, generator backup |
| Operational capability gaps | Regular ToT and technical support model |
| Inconsistent utilization practices | SOP enforcement and monitoring review |
| Financial sustainability pressure | OSR model and multi-partner engagement |

---

## 8. Expected Outcomes
1. BDSET centres remain fully functional as national digital skill hubs.  
2. TCMS-based standardized management improves transparency and accountability.  
3. Increased employability and practical skills pipeline in emerging technologies.  
4. Stronger innovation ecosystem and collaboration opportunities.  
5. Long-term sustainability through structured governance and revenue mechanisms.

---

## 9. Key Recommendations for Approval
1. Continue centre operations under structured BHTPA supervision.  
2. Approve TCMS as unified operational platform.  
3. Ensure 24/7 infrastructure support for GPU/server operations.  
4. Adopt and enforce SOP for all centre assets and processes.  
5. Enable OSR-based sustainability initiatives.  
6. Prioritize AI and Immersive Technology streams as strategic skills domain.  
7. Encourage research and innovation network with institutions/industry.  
8. Support district-level job fairs and technology seminar linkage.

---

## 10. Conclusion
This proposal provides a practical pathway to convert BDSET from a completed project into a sustained national capability platform. With formal approval and structured implementation, the centres will continue to deliver measurable value in training, employment, innovation, and digital transformation.
