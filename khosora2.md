## Slide 2: Presentation Purpose
**Purpose of This Proposal**
- Ensure long-term operation of BDSET centres after project completion
- Institutionalize existing assets, systems, and training capacity
- Build a measurable model for skills-to-employment outcomes
- Align centre operations with BHTPA’s national digital development goals

---

## Slide 3: Current Project Status
**What Has Been Established**
- 6 BDSET centres are operational
- Approximately 400+ trainees trained in immersive technology streams
- Modern hardware ecosystem deployed across centres
- Core focus areas: AI, VR, DL, ML, XR, AR

---

## Slide 4: Existing Strategic Assets
**Infrastructure and Technology Base**
- DL Mini Super Computers (NVIDIA RTX A4000)
- VR Mini Super Computers (NVIDIA Quadro GV100)
- Desktop labs, laptops, VR headsets
- Linux and Windows server environment
- Interactive learning infrastructure (interactive board, digital podium)

---

## Slide 5: Why Sustainability Now
**Strategic Need for Post-Project Continuity**
- High-value infrastructure must remain operational and productive
- Training pipelines must continue under institutional governance
- TCMS should become a standardized operating platform
- Employment and innovation outcomes require long-cycle support

---

## Slide 6: Proposed Sustainability Model
**Four-Pillar Operating Framework**
1. Infrastructure Activation (GPU/Server/Lab Utilization)
2. TCMS Institutionalization (Management + Reporting)
3. Employment & Entrepreneurship Integration
4. Governance, SOP, and Revenue Sustainability (OSR)

---

## Slide 7: GPU & Compute Utilization Strategy
**High-Value Resource Optimization**
- Use GPU resources for AI/HPC training and applied learning
- Support research and advanced practical modules
- Enable government/public-use case data processing support
- Strengthen inter-agency collaboration pathways (including technical coordination)

---

## Slide 8: TCMS as National Operations Backbone
**Institutional Digital Management**
- Batch planning, attendance, assessment, reporting in one platform
- ToT-led capability development for centre teams
- Data-driven monitoring and performance review
- Standardized management model across all BDSET centres

---

## Slide 9: Training Continuity Framework
**Structured Programme Streams**
- Government and non-government officer training
- Job-oriented digital capacity tracks
- Advanced immersive technology learning tracks
- Professional readiness modules (including freelancing pathways)

---

## Slide 10: Employment Integration Pathways
**From Training to Livelihood**
- Domestic employer linkage and job fair ecosystem
- Sector-specific placement collaboration
- Skill database-based candidate mapping
- Market-aligned competency preparation for overseas opportunities

---

## Slide 11: Entrepreneurship & Innovation Strategy
**Future-Ready Innovation Channel**
- AI and immersive-tech startup enablement
- Incubation, mentorship, and ecosystem partnerships
- Public-private collaboration for scale-up
- Innovation labs as local economic multipliers

---

## Slide 12: International Collaboration & Exchange
**Global Exposure and Capability Building**
- International Training Exchange Program activation
- Joint certification opportunities
- Cross-border research and innovation projects
- Internship/fellowship channels and global trainer pool development

---

## Slide 13: Governance & Compliance Architecture
**Operational Control and Accountability**
- Lead oversight under BHTPA
- Clear role assignment and review cycle
- SOP-based asset utilization and maintenance governance
- Periodic reporting and centre performance dashboard

---

## Slide 14: Phased Implementation Roadmap
**Phase 1 (0–6 Months)**
- Handover, responsibility assignment, TCMS onboarding, ToT

**Phase 2 (6–12 Months)**
- Full training calendar, institutional utilization, job linkage activities

**Phase 3 (1–3 Years)**
- OSR stabilization, innovation scale-up, international partnership expansion

---

## Slide 15: KPI & Monitoring Matrix
**Core Performance Indicators**
- Active centres (operational readiness)
- Trainee throughput and completion
- TCMS usage and reporting consistency
- Employment and engagement linkage indicators
- Infrastructure uptime and compliance score

---

## Slide 16: Risk and Mitigation
**Primary Risks**
- Power instability and downtime
- Operational skill gaps
- Process inconsistency
- Long-term financing pressure

**Mitigation Actions**
- Backup power + preventive maintenance
- Regular ToT and technical support
- SOP enforcement and review controls
- OSR and partnership-based sustainability model

---

## Slide 17: Approval Requests
**Requested Decisions from BHTPA**
- Approve formal post-project operating framework
- Endorse TCMS as the unified management platform
- Authorize SOP-governed utilization model for all centres
- Approve phased KPI-based monitoring mechanism
- Approve OSR-oriented sustainability pathway

---

## Slide 18: Conclusion
**Strategic Closing Statement**
BDSET centres can evolve from project assets into a sustained national capability platform. With formal approval and structured implementation, the ecosystem will continue to generate value through skill development, innovation, employment linkage, and digital transformation support.
